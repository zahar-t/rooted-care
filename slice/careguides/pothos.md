# Pothos (Epipremnum aureum) — Sofia's care guide

**At a glance:** the most forgiving plant we ship. Tolerates low light, forgives missed waterings, trails beautifully.

**Light:** anything from low light to bright indirect. Variegation fades in deep shade.

**Water:** water when the top half of the pot is dry. Droopy + dry soil = thirsty; droopy + wet soil = overwatered. It bounces back from underwatering within hours of a drink.

**Common problems:**
- *Yellow leaves* — usually overwatering; let it dry out properly between drinks.
- *Brown leaf tips* — tap water minerals or very dry air; not serious.
- *Leggy vines* — normal; trim and the cuttings root in water in about two weeks.

**Pets:** ⚠️ toxic to cats and dogs (insoluble calcium oxalates). Chewing causes mouth/tongue irritation, drooling, pawing at the mouth, sometimes vomiting. It is very rarely life-threatening, but if an animal has chewed it: remove plant material from the mouth, offer water, and ring your vet — especially if vomiting, swelling, or breathing changes. Keep pothos hanging or on a high shelf in pet households.
