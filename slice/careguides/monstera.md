# Monstera deliciosa — Sofia's care guide

**At a glance:** bright indirect light, water when the top 5cm of soil is dry, loves humidity, grows fast in summer.

**Light:** bright but indirect. Direct midday sun scorches the leaves (bleached, crispy patches). Too little light = small leaves with no splits.

**Water:** the #1 killer is overwatering. Water thoroughly when the top 5cm of soil is dry — usually every 7–10 days in summer, every 2–3 weeks in winter. Always let excess drain; never leave it sitting in a wet saucer.

**Common problems:**
- *Yellow lower leaves* — almost always overwatering, occasionally natural shedding of the oldest leaf or two. Check the soil: if it's damp below the surface, let it dry out fully before the next water and it usually self-corrects. One or two old leaves yellowing on an otherwise happy plant is normal.
- *Brown crispy edges* — dry air or inconsistent watering. Mist or move near other plants.
- *No leaf splits* — needs more light. Splits come with maturity and good light.

**Pets:** toxic to cats and dogs if chewed (insoluble calcium oxalates) — mouth irritation, drooling, vomiting. Rarely dangerous but keep out of reach and call a vet if symptoms persist.
