# Calathea — Sofia's care guide

**At a glance:** the drama queen of the box. Stunning patterned leaves, fussy about water quality and humidity. Pet-safe!

**Light:** medium indirect light. Direct sun fades the patterns.

**Water:** keep lightly moist (never soggy), and use filtered or rainwater if you can — calatheas hate the fluoride/chlorine in tap water. Water when the top 2cm is dry.

**Common problems:**
- *Brown crispy edges or patches* — the classic calathea complaint: tap water minerals and/or dry air. Switch to filtered water and raise humidity (pebble tray, grouping plants, or a bathroom shelf). Damaged leaves don't recover — trim them and judge progress by the NEW growth.
- *Curling leaves* — thirsty or air too dry.
- *Leaves fold up at night* — completely normal! It's called nyctinasty; they pray at night.

**Pets:** non-toxic to cats and dogs. One of the reasons we love shipping them.

**Note for transit:** calatheas sulk after shipping. Some browning within the first week of arrival is common and usually cosmetic — new growth is the real health indicator.
