# Snake plant (Sansevieria / Dracaena trifasciata) — Sofia's care guide

**At a glance:** nearly indestructible. Tolerates neglect, low light, and forgetful owners. The only way to kill it is kindness.

**Light:** anything short of a dark cupboard. Grows faster in bright light.

**Water:** every 3–6 weeks. Let the soil dry out COMPLETELY between waterings. In winter, once a month is plenty.

**Common problems:**
- *Mushy, soft leaves at the base* — overwatering / root rot. Stop watering, remove mushy leaves, repot in dry gritty soil if it spreads.
- *Wrinkled, bending leaves* — very thirsty (this takes months of neglect).
- *Brown tips* — cosmetic; usually cold drafts or erratic watering.

**Pets:** mildly toxic to cats and dogs if chewed (saponins) — can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea. Usually mild and self-limiting, but mention it to your vet if symptoms don't settle.
